# Act

A minimal, local-first intervention tool that breaks impulsive behavior loops in real time.

Built for the moment you’re about to break your own rules.

---

## Why this exists

Most productivity tools help when you're calm.

They assume:

* you can think clearly
* you’ll follow your plan
* you have control

That’s not when failure happens.

Failure happens in short windows:

* impulse hits
* autopilot takes over
* you act before thinking

Act is built specifically for that moment.

---

## Core concept

Act does not track habits.
It intervenes.

When an urge appears:

1. You trigger the app
2. The app interrupts you
3. It forces a single action
4. It keeps pressure until you either act or fail

The goal is simple:

> delay the impulse long enough for it to lose power

---

## How it works

### 1. Trigger

Press:

**ACT**

This signals:

> “I’m about to break my system”

---

### 2. Interrupt

* Fullscreen takeover
* Strong directive messaging
* Sound + visual disruption

Purpose:

* break autopilot
* force awareness

---

### 3. Commit

You confirm action:

> “I’M GOING NOW”

This shifts:

* intention → commitment

---

### 4. Execution

* 10-minute timer starts immediately
* clear instruction:

> MAKE EGGS NOW
> GO TO KITCHEN

No alternatives. No decision-making.

---

### 5. Check-ins

Every few minutes:

> DID YOU START?

You must respond:

* YES
* NO

---

### 6. Escalation

If you answer **NO**:

* the app pushes back
* increases pressure
* redirects you to act

No passive failure.

---

### 7. Outcome

At the end:

* I ate from my stack
* I bought junk

Result is logged locally.

---

## Design philosophy

Everything is built around:

* speed

* force

---

## What this is NOT

* Not a habit tracker
* Not a todo app
* Not a motivational tool

It does not help you plan.

It helps you **when your plan is about to fail**.

---

## Target use

* impulsive eating
* breaking discipline loops
* short-term loss of control

Any scenario where:

> you act before thinking
